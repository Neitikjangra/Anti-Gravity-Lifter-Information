Technology Framework

The scientific foundation and implementation of our electrostatic anti-gravity research

Biefeld-Brown Effect Implementation

Our research builds upon the Biefeld-Brown effect, where high voltage applied between asymmetric electrodes creates ionic wind that generates thrust. Through rigorous experimentation, we've developed several key innovations:

  • Optimized electrode geometry that maximizes ionic wind generation while minimizing corona discharge losses
  • Novel dielectric materials that withstand higher voltages without breakdown
  • Precision voltage modulation techniques that improve energy efficiency by 42%
  • Machine learning algorithms that predict and adjust for atmospheric conditions

    • The system consists of thin tungsten wire electrodes (emitters) and larger aluminum foil electrodes (collectors) arranged in a carefully calibrated asymmetric configuration. Our proprietary three-dimensional electrode arrangement has demonstrated thrust efficiencies 320% greater than conventional planar designs.

    Academic papers and presentations from our six-month research project

    Optimization of Electrode Geometry for Enhanced Biefeld-Brown Effect

    Kartik Tiwari, Rajarshi Shukla, Neitik Jangid March 2023 Electrostatic Propulsion

    This paper presents our findings on electrode geometry optimization, demonstrating how asymmetric 3D arrangements can improve thrust efficiency by up to 320% compared to traditional planar designs. Includes detailed CAD models and experimental results.

    Machine Learning Approaches for High-Voltage Electrostatic Thrust Control

    Neitik Jangid, Kartik Tiwari April 2023 AI Applications

    Details our implementation of neural networks to predict and adjust voltage parameters in real-time based on environmental conditions, achieving 18% better efficiency than static voltage systems.

    Download PDF View Online

    Material Science Considerations for High-Voltage Electrostatic Lifters

    Rajarshi Shukla, Kartik Tiwari May 2023 Materials Engineering

    Comparative analysis of electrode and dielectric materials for electrostatic propulsion systems, with test results for aluminum, copper, and gold-plated electrodes under various atmospheric conditions.
